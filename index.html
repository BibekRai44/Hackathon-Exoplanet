PLANET INFO

Mass, Size & Density

Our planet, Neozoi-151 has several unique characteristics that set it apart from Earth and makes it an intriguing celestial body. With a mass of approximately 9.85* 1024 kg, the planet is 1.7 times heavier than Earth. It has a radius of 8,657 kilometers, which is approximately 1.35 times the Earth’s radius. The planet boasts a density of 3.623 grams per cubic centimeter, significantly denser than our home planet. This high density suggests that it likely has a substantial core of heavy elements, making it distinct in terms of its internal composition.

Gravity

Gravity on this planet is measured at 8.82 meters per second squared (m/s^2), which is approximately 0.9 times the gravity on Earth. This slightly weaker gravity would have implications for the planet's atmosphere, allowing it to potentially retain gases that might escape a planet with higher gravity.

Distance from Earth and its Star

The planet orbits its star at a distance of 0.9 astronomical units (AU) which lies in the habitable zone of the star. The planet is located at a staggering distance of 561.57 light years from Earth, making it an exoplanet beyond our reach for direct exploration.

Climate and Air Composition

In terms of its climate, the planet experiences a very cold temperature range, varying from -10°C to 10°C. This range suggests a climate that might support liquid water, a crucial ingredient for the potential development of life as we know it. Moreover, the air of this planet is composed 19% of oxygen, further the presence of 1.3 times Earth's greenhouse gases hints at a potential greenhouse effect, which could further impact the planet's climate.

Rotation and Revolution

The planet rotates relatively slowly with a rotation period of 36 Earth hours, while its revolution period around its star is 336 earth days or approximately 0.9208 Earth years. This means that a day on this planet is much longer than on Earth, potentially affecting its weather patterns and day-night cycles.

Reflection of Light of the Star

With an albedo of 0.26, the planet reflects a moderate amount of its star’s light, which could influence its overall temperature distribution and climate.
